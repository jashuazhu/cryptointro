More about Stablecoin

A stablecoin is a specialized category of cryptocurrency engineered to maintain a stable value and consistent purchasing power over time. Unlike typical cryptocurrencies that experience significant price swings, stablecoins are designed to act as a reliable medium of exchange and a store of value within the digital ecosystem.

Mechanisms

To achieve this stability, they utilize several primary mechanisms:

  • Asset Pegging: Their value is linked to a specific asset or a basket of assets, most commonly fiat currency (like the U.S. dollar), commodities (like gold), or other cryptocurrencies.
  • Collateralization: Many are reserve-backed, meaning the issuer holds a pool of assets (e.g., Treasury bonds or bank deposits) that allow the token to be redeemable for the underlying value.
  • Algorithmic Stabilization: Some "synthetic" or algorithmic stablecoins rely on computer protocols to dynamically manage the token supply to maintain their price peg without a full physical reserve.
